Reach of the path
A long solid ribbon means openpilot is sure of the road well ahead. As confidence drops the ribbon dissolves and pulls back toward the car — you see trust shorten before anything goes wrong.
Steering strain
In a hard curve the strained side edge of the path brightens as openpilot nears its steering-torque limit — an early, directional cue instead of an alert after the wheel gives out.
Braking strain + lead
A chevron marks the car ahead. As openpilot brakes toward its authority limit, a tension band grows back from the lead — the fore-aft mirror of steering strain. Hard acceleration shows as forward surge chevrons.
Hand-off + sound
Only when a hand-off is likely does a calm edge-breath, a plain prompt, and a soft reserved chime appear. Silence means trust — so the sound, when it comes, actually means something.

Driver attention — restraint over shouting

openpilot already knows when your eyes drift — 0.11.1 even warms the driver-monitoring icon before any alert fires. But today that path still ends in a full-screen shout. The same signal can be handled as a calm, earlier, graduated nudge that never hijacks the screen. Drag the attention slider and compare the two.
